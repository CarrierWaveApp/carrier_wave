Executive Summary

Carrier Wave is a SwiftUI/SwiftData iOS app for amateur radio QSO logging with cloud sync to QRZ, POTA, Ham2K LoFi, LoTW, Club Log, and HAMRS. This report analyzes the competitive landscape, user sentiment across major amateur radio communities, and emerging trends to identify high-impact feature gaps and strategic opportunities.

Key finding: Carrier Wave already leads the market in CW transcription, WebSDR recording, activation statistics depth, and sync service breadth. The highest-ROI opportunities are in hunter award tracking, contest logging, propagation-aware band suggestions, and offline callsign lookup — features no mobile competitor does well.

1. Competitive Landscape

Primary Mobile Competitors

Ham2K PoLo (Portable Logger)

Platforms: iOS, Android (React Native, open source)  |  Price: Free

Fastest-growing app in the POTA/SOTA community. Supports POTA, SOTA, WWFF, GMA, Field Day, and World Castles simultaneously. Auto self-spotting every 10 minutes, excellent P2P/S2S workflow, offline park/summit data, multi-language (7+ languages). Cloud sync (LoFi) still in alpha. Strong developer-community engagement.

"I've run 1000s of contacts through half a dozen logging apps and nothing compares with the simplicity and feature-rich logging experience of PoLo."

Weaknesses: No desktop version. Cloud sync alpha-quality. No LoTW integration. No rig control. No contest scoring. Limited award tracking.

HAMRS / HAMRS Pro

Platforms: iOS, macOS, Windows, Linux  |  Price: $4.99 + $2/month (Pro sync)

Multi-platform with templates for POTA, SOTA, Field Day. FLRig integration for CAT control. WSJT-X integration. Long track record as the original POTA logger. Removed from Google Play (Android) in 2025.

Weaknesses: Stalled development periods. No LoTW. QSO map reported broken. Many users migrating to PoLo.

World Radio League (WRL)

Platforms: iOS, Android, Web  |  Price: Free

Modern UI targeting new/young operators. Cloud sync, offline-capable. Auto-generated QSL cards for every contact. WRTC 2026 partnership for live contest scoring. 200k+ user reach via Ham Radio Prep pipeline.

Weaknesses: Limited award tracking. No rig control. No LoTW. No SOTA-specific features.

HamLog (Pignology)

Platforms: iOS, Android, Web  |  Price: $0.99

Longest track record on iOS. Extensive built-in tools (maps, band plans, calculators, DX cluster). Rig control via Pigtail hardware. UI feels dated. Not designed for rapid activation logging.

Desktop & Specialized Competitors

Desktop loggers (N1MM, Log4OM, HRD, RUMlogNG, MacLoggerDX) dominate contesting and award tracking but have no mobile presence. Wavelog (self-hosted web, open source) is growing among technically inclined operators who want data ownership. HunterLog (desktop) is purpose-built for POTA/SOTA hunters with CAT control. FT HAM (iOS) is a breakthrough — full FT8/FT4 decode on iPhone.

3. Critical Feature Gaps

3.1   Contest Logging Templates

HIGH IMPACT MEDIUM EFFORT

No mobile logger does this well. Field Day, Winter Field Day, and state QSO parties are massive participation events — many operators activate POTA parks during them simultaneously.

Required capabilities:

  • Session templates for Field Day (1D/2D class + ARRL section exchange), WFD, state QSO parties
  • Dupe checking per contest rules (band/mode combinations vary by contest)
  • Exchange fields (serial number, section, county, etc.)
  • Score calculation and summary display
  • Cabrillo export alongside ADIF

WRL has live contest leaderboards via their WRTC 2026 partnership but no real scoring engine. N1MM dominates desktop but has zero mobile presence. This is wide open on mobile.

3.2   WWFF / GMA / Additional Activity Programs

MEDIUM-HIGH IMPACT LOW EFFORT

Ham2K PoLo supports WWFF (World Wide Flora & Fauna), GMA (Global Mountain Activity), and World Castles Awards. International operators expect this. The architecture for park-based activations already exists in Carrier Wave — extending to WWFF references and GMA summits is incremental work on the data model and lookup services.

3.3   eQSL Integration

MEDIUM IMPACT LOW EFFORT

Not currently in the sync services. eQSL has a large user base, especially among casual operators and those who value visual QSL cards. The upload API is a straightforward ADIF POST request.

3.4   LoTW Upload (Not Just Download)

VERY HIGH IMPACT HIGH EFFORT

The single biggest universal gap in mobile logging. No mobile app uploads to LoTW — TQSL is desktop-only and requires private key signing. ARRL has shown no interest in a mobile API.

Possible approaches:

  • Queue uploads and push through a companion Mac/PC agent
  • Embed TQSL signing logic in iOS (TQSL source is open source — investigate feasibility)
  • Partner with ARRL on a mobile signing flow

Even a partial solution would be a headline feature and significant competitive advantage.

3.5   Needed References Tracker (Hunter Award Chasing)

HIGH IMPACT MEDIUM EFFORT

The hunter side is underserved across all apps. Operators chasing awards (all 50 states, DXCC, all POTA parks in a state) need:

  • A "needed" list showing which parks/states/entities/grids they still lack
  • Spot alerts filtered to "I need this one" (think HamAlert-style integration)
  • Visual progress toward specific awards (POTA Kilo, All States, DXCC, etc.)
  • Badge on spots when a station is in a needed park/state/entity

The Activity Log already has spots and worked-before badges. Adding a "needed" dimension would transform it into a proper hunter tool.

3.6   Propagation-Aware Band Suggestions

MEDIUM-HIGH IMPACT MEDIUM EFFORT

Carrier Wave already displays solar conditions (K-index, SFI, A-index, band outlook from HamQSL). The next step is actionable advice in the logging screen:

  • "20m open to EU right now" based on real-time conditions and operator location
  • Band recommendation when starting a session based on time of day + solar conditions
  • Color-code bands in the band picker by current propagation quality
  • Leverage existing spot data — "12 stations spotted on 20m in last 10 min, 0 on 40m"

3.7   Offline Callsign Database

HIGH IMPACT MEDIUM EFFORT

Remote parks and all SOTA summits typically have no cell service. The current callsign lookup is two-tier (Polo notes + QRZ API), but QRZ requires network. Shipping a compressed FCC ULS database (or HamDB/HamQTH dump) for offline lookup would ensure the callsign card always populates. The full FCC database is ~2M records but compresses well — or offer a regional/national subset download.

3.8   Satellite Operation Logging

LOW-MEDIUM IMPACT LOW EFFORT

A small but dedicated community. Required: OSCAR designator field, uplink/downlink frequency pair, satellite name/number. Ham2K PoLo has this. Low effort to add the fields to the existing QSO model.

4. Adjustments to Existing Features

4.1   Internationalization

HIGH IMPACT (GROWTH) HIGH EFFORT

PoLo has 7+ language translations and growing international adoption. Carrier Wave's band plan and license class validation appear US-centric (Technician/General/Extra). International operators need:

  • IARU Region 1/2/3 band plans
  • Country-specific license class validation
  • Localized UI strings (start with Spanish, German, Japanese — largest non-English ham populations)
  • Non-US callsign format handling in validation
  • Support for regional activation programs (VKFF in Australia, BOTA in UK, etc.)

4.2   Home Screen & Lock Screen Widgets

MEDIUM IMPACT MEDIUM EFFORT

HAM-Toolbox (iOS) proves operator demand for glanceable data. WidgetKit targets:

  • Solar conditions (SFI / K-index / A-index) — small widget
  • Current streak count — small widget
  • Daily QSO counter — small widget
  • "Band conditions right now" — medium widget
  • Next POTA spot timer — Lock Screen complication

4.3   Voice Input for Callsign Entry

MEDIUM-HIGH IMPACT MEDIUM EFFORT

Repeatedly requested across communities. Operating with cold hands, holding a mic, or managing antennas makes typing difficult. iOS Speech framework supports continuous dictation. The challenge is mapping NATO phonetic alphabet to characters ("Whiskey Six Juliet Sierra Victor" → W6JSV). A phonetic-to-character mapper or fine-tuned recognition model would enable hands-free logging.

4.4   Apple Watch Companion

MEDIUM IMPACT MEDIUM-HIGH EFFORT

Quick-glance information during field operations: current frequency/mode, QSO count, last logged call, streak status, and a "log contact" complication for the simplest QSOs. Useful when the phone is in a pocket or mounted away from the operator.

5. Emerging Opportunities

5.1   Meshtastic Integration for Offline Spotting

NICHE BUT GROWING MEDIUM EFFORT

Meshtastic (LoRa mesh networking) is exploding in the ham community. At field events and remote activations, a Meshtastic mesh could serve as a spotting backchannel when cell service is unavailable. SOTAmāt already encodes spots as audio tones — Meshtastic over Bluetooth LE is more elegant.

Use cases: Coordinate with nearby activators, share spots in group activations, emergency status communication.

5.2   AI-Assisted Logging from Audio

HIGH FUTURE IMPACT HIGH EFFORT

No competitor does this. The building blocks exist within Carrier Wave:

  • CW transcription already extracts callsigns from audio
  • Extend to SSB voice: iOS speech recognition → callsign extraction → auto-populate log fields
  • "Hearing" the other station say their callsign, grid, name, and RST could auto-fill the QSO form
  • Even partial automation (suggest callsign, operator confirms) saves significant keystrokes

This is R&D territory but would be a generational feature — no competitor is close.

5.3   CAT / Rig Control via Bluetooth or WiFi

HIGH IMPACT HIGH EFFORT

The IC-705 (WiFi), KX2/KX3 (Bluetooth), and Yaesu FT-710 (USB) are the most popular portable rigs. Auto-populating frequency/mode/power from the radio eliminates manual entry errors. SDR-Control Mobile (iOS, $45) proves rig control works on iPhone. Even supporting just the IC-705 via WiFi would cover a large portion of portable operators.

5.4   FT8/FT4 Decode Integration

MEDIUM-HIGH IMPACT VERY HIGH EFFORT

FT HAM (iOS) proves native FT8 decoding is possible on iPhone. This is a major engineering effort but would make Carrier Wave the first app combining traditional logging + CW decode + digital mode decode. A more practical near-term step: import from WSJT-X via network (UDP) when operating portable with a laptop + phone setup.

6. Accessibility & Underserved Demographics

Visually Impaired & Blind Operators

An active community exists (Blind Hams Network, CQ Blind Hams podcast, Courage Kenny Handiham Program). Radio equipment is "surprisingly accessible, even for totally blind operators" but software is the weak link. The WSJT-X accessibility initiative (2025–2026) aims to make digital modes usable for blind operators. Ham2K PoLo recently added accessibility improvements.

Opportunity: Comprehensive VoiceOver support, high-contrast modes, large touch targets, and audio feedback for log entry confirmation. This is a differentiator almost no competitor invests in meaningfully.

Motor Disabilities

19.9 million Americans have difficulty lifting or grasping. Voice input for logging is the most-requested accessibility feature. Large touch targets, simplified field entry, and Switch Control support (iOS Accessibility framework) would serve this population directly.

New & Young Operators

Common complaint from younger operators about logging software: "So feature dense, developed in vacuums, hard to use, buggy, and lacking in UX." World Radio League specifically targets this demographic through Ham Radio Prep (200k+ reach). Young operators want modern UI, social features, gamification, quick setup, no jargon.

Carrier Wave's tours and onboarding system is already strong — continue investing in progressive disclosure and guided first experiences.

International Operators

US-centric design is a top-10 complaint. Band plans, callsign formats, and regulatory assumptions vary by country. Multi-language support (PoLo leads with 7+) and IARU region-aware band plans are table stakes for international growth.

9. Strategic Summary

Carrier Wave occupies a strong position in the mobile amateur radio logging market. Its CW transcription, WebSDR recording, professional statistics, and six-service sync portfolio are genuine differentiators that no competitor matches.

The primary growth vectors are:

Near-term (high ROI): Hunter award tracking, contest templates, propagation-aware suggestions, offline callsign database, WWFF/GMA support, eQSL sync. These build on existing architecture and fill the most-reported user gaps.
Medium-term (growth): Internationalization, widgets, voice input, Apple Watch. These expand the addressable market and improve field usability.
Long-term (transformative): LoTW mobile upload, IC-705 rig control, AI voice-to-log, FT8 decode. These require significant investment but would establish category leadership.

The competitive landscape is fragmented — PoLo leads on simplicity and international support, WRL on modern UI and social, HAMRS on cross-platform reach. No single app excels across all dimensions. Carrier Wave's depth-first approach (professional statistics, CW decode, WebSDR) is a viable differentiation strategy, especially when combined with the breadth additions recommended above.
